To some life on the boat might seem a bit slow but there are some issues that take a bit more time and focus.  Like the issue of do we sail down to Trinidad and Tobago or not?  That seems like a simple question but you need to understand the issues around it to fully appreciate the problem.

We have always looked to head to Trinidad if there was a hurricane that came close to Grenada.  The number of hurricanes that have gone thru Trinidad are zero so it makes a spot to go if there is a hurricane.  The negative of staying in Trinidad is it is HOT.... HOT.....HOT.  At least that is what we have heard.  The water in the Trinidad area is not pleasant so jumping in to the water to cool off is just not an option.  The other issue is for Sam, by Trinidad law, he would have to be quarantined for six months.  Several cruisers will keep their pets on board so they do not have to worry about it.  And we have heard other cruisers that take the dog and just walk them in the marina, or on a deserted beach in the morning, and have had no issues, but technically, it is against the law.

With that why would we consider even going?  Well the information that we have heard is the food, fuel, and work/labor is cheaper there than anywhere else.  Since we are looking to resupply our food inventory we thought that going to Trinidad might be just the ticket.  The boat does not need any major work so the need for work/labor is not needed.  To deal with the hotness of the area we would go to a marina and hook up the AC on the boat and would stay somewhat comfortable.  The issue there, is that the only work the boat does need, is one A/C unit is not working.  We have one that does, but one doesn't, so if we went to Trinidad to the dock with power, then we should fix the other unit, but if we don't go, we don't need the A/C and don't have to fix it.   The other issue would still be how do we keep Sam happy and walked.

To top those issues we also had another boat that was going to meet us there.  The sail down to Trinidad is 90 miles so a day/night trip would be needed.  It would be nice to see the other boat and spend some time exploring the area, we could also go out to Tobago Island if we were in the area.

Thus our dilemma, go to Trinidad or not.

End result: we stayed in Grenada
