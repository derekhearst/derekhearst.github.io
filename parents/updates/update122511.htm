Southern Florida along the ICW shows a lot of wealthy people, their homes and boats.  The homes seem to be in the 5 to 10 millions and then the boats in front of the house is another 2 to 4 million.  There are a few that are well over that but it is not an area for the poor.  Currently we are anchored in Northern Palm Beach and in the next couple of days we will head to Miami.  We have heard that you have to go to South Beach and see the sites as well.  From Miami it is an easier crossing to Bahamas and you do not have to fight the Gulf Stream, the 2 to 4 knot current that runs north along the Florida coast.

The boat is about ready, we did have to replace the depth instrument, it was original so it is not too surprising.  The sails, motor, and now the water maker are all running smoothly and ready to go.  We have been provisioning and with the water line running a bit lower we are stocked up with food for the next 3 to 5 months.  Well at least the stables are on board and we will add fresh fruits and such as we pull into different stops.  We have also heard that paper products are expensive in the islands as well so there is a large supply in the forward berth.

At the last anchorage Derek caught the mighty cat fish what had been bothering the dogs.  Ok so the fish did not bother the dogs but Derek did catch a nice 14 inch cat fish that we returned to the wild.
