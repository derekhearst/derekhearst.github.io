4/10/11 update

Old steering radial drive that needed to be cut out due to corrosion.  Underneath this was  a corroded rudder post bearing.

That simple project of replacing the steering cable grease fitting is done.  Replacing the grease fitting was easy, it was getting the old steering quadrant off, taking off the corroded rudder bearing, installing the new bearing, resetting the cables and then getting both rudders aligned that took the time.  I think someone use to say "if you are going to do a job well then do it 2 or 3, even 4 times".  It seemed like we would get 90% of something done and then have to redo some of the work just to get back to the same completion level.  Well after two new bearings, a new quadrant, several new drill bits and even a new drill the steering system looks to be 100% ready to go.  Now it will be getting the auto pilot and other miscellaneous items reinstalled and this project might be "done".

Kris has also been working on the electrical system, our batteries were older than 5 years and had been dry when we purchased the boat.  We replaced the Trojan 105s with new Trojan 105 plus and even went with 8 batteries vs. the 6 that we had to begin with.  New combiners for the engine and house batteries was also added.  The wiring was not as straight forward as we wanted so now with a new electrical diagram the system is getting close to done.  We have the two new Kyocera 135 solar panels to add to our 4 Kyocera 80 panels with the Blue Sky controller to make sure everything works together and we should be set for awhile.

Its like a maze of wires.
