"What If" is hull number 7 and is an Endeavour built Manta.  The original owners took possession in 1995, and sailed extensively in the Chesapeake and surrounds.  The next owners purchased the boat in 2000, and sailed the east coast and the Bahamas.  They sold the boat in 2008 and the next owners lived aboard at dock for one year, then the boat sat anchored until the current owners purchased her in 2010.  They went through the boat completely and did many upgrades to prepare for cruising the boat.  They took the boat from Rhode Island to Trinidad and now back on a three year cruise.  This boat is ready to go again and can take you anywhere you wish to go safely and comfortably.

Accommodations and Layout:
You will notice the Manta feels more like a home than a charter cat where the main cabin is filled with the table for maximum seating. As you walk into the cabin from the cockpit, you will notice a Lexan locking door with screen.  There is a oval table and seating for six comfortably. The dinette has a custom table that can be raised or lowered.  There are two moveable seats with storage.  Under the dinette seating are the batteries, one air-conditioning unit and the refrigeration unit. There are large window for excellent visibility that come with Phifertex screens for the hot days.  There are two screened hatches in the main cabin, one fan by the galley, and one moveable fan that plugs into one of the four 12 volt outlets located in the main cabin.  The floor is teak and holly plywood. The walls and cabinets are white laminate offset by teak trim. 

There is a  U-shaped galley with upper and lower cabinets, a Seward Princess three burner stove and oven.  A double stainless sink with gooseneck faucet and soap dispenser.  The refrigerator/freezer are very large with excellent insulation powered by a Nova Kool air cooled 12 volt unit.   There is a teak paper towel holder, three lights for great illumination.  There is a microwave locker with its own 110 plug, and in the galley are two more 110 plugs plus a 12 volt plug.

Also in the main cabin is a  wet locker and t.v. cabinet.  There is a built in Clarion stereo/cd player with two speakers in the main cabin, and more speakers in the cockpit. 

A chart table with a lift top has ample storage, chart light and two repeater Autohelm instruments for wind/depth, and gps coordinates.  Under the chart table is the electrical panel with easy access to all wiring.  A 200 watt inverter charger and two solar panel regulators are located here as well.

This boat comes equipped with air-conditioning.  It has two CruisAir units. 

The Manta layout has a "master" side on the port side with a large stateroom, with queen size bed,  a double cedar lined closet, four pull out drawers, plus under seat storage, bookshelf and two more storage lockers.
Mid boat on the port side is a large pantry, and a 5' long bookshelf/storage area. 
Forward is a large master bath with a large separate shower, manual toilet and sink with a large mirrored  storage.  

On the starboard side there is a large stateroom aft, with a queen size bed, bookshelf, cedar lined closet, two pullout drawers and under seat storage as well.
Mid boat on starboard is a smaller bookshelf, and a head with small shower, electric toilet and sink. 
Forward on starboard there is a smaller bed, that is twin sized on one end, and larger than a double on the other.  A large closet, under bed storage, and under seat storage.  

All cabins and head have teak louvered doors, fans in all cabins and heads, 12 volt outlets and 110 outlets.  Each cabin has its own screened opening hatch and screened opening ports.  The port head has a hatch and port as well, and the starboard head has an opening screened port.    Each bed has a hypervent base to prevent mold and allow airflow.
